Exterior Power Washing Solutions

Project Summary

Exterior Power Washing Solutions is a business website that needed a makeover. The client felt that the website did not reflect the look of the company and that the information was not very clear to potential customers. I set out to create a website that was simple, navigable, and would increase new business inquiries.

The Client

Exterior Power Washing Solutions business owner

Timeframe

Two weeks

My Role

As the sole UX Designer, I worked on user interviews, information architecture, creating low-fidelity wireframes, creating high fidelity page mockups and prototypes for the website.

Tools/Methods

User Research, Personas, Wireframes, Prototypes, Sketch App, WordPress, HTML, CSS, JavaScript

Project Process

User Research

I began the project by speaking with homeowners about how they perform outdoor home maintenance and/or other services. I conducted 4 informal interviews and chose participants of different ages and genders. The biggest challenge of the project was that I had no commercial business owner to interview and limited time/resources to find the participants.

Although the interviews were informal, the following questions were asked in every interview:

  • How do you usually get home maintenance done?
  • If you have to find someone to perform a service, where do you start looking?
  • How do you usually contact businesses?
  • What makes you feel confident in a business doing a great job?

User Personas

From the information gathered from the interviews, I created the primary persona to highlight the most consistent points provided by the users.

The Old Website

Since I did not have access to current website analytics data, I started out by wireframing the current homepage as well as creating a sitemap with all the current content. I concluded that the main issues were as follows:

  • Unclear hierarchy since links to other pages were scattered across the homepage.
  • The picture in the hero area did not reflect the business.
  • Calls-to-action were not distinguishable between the other links on the page.
  • Important information was not readily available and could be consolidated from other pages.

Sketching and Wireframing

After discussing with the client, I decided to consolidate the pages and place certain information the main homepage. The new site map was set up with less pages. A few sketches were made on where to place certain elemends based on the user insight.
